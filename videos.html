Week 1: Introduction to Self-Introduction

In the first week, I was introduced to the importance of self-introduction. I understood how a clear and confident self-intro helps set the tone in interviews, group discussions, and professional meetings. I practiced speaking about myself including basic details like my name, the place I come from, my educational background, and my family. This gave me a foundation to build more structured and impactful communication in the coming weeks.

Week 2: Five Ring Theory of Self-Introduction

During Week 2, I learned about the Five Ring Theory, which breaks down a good self-introduction into five key parts: Name, Place, Education, Family, and Goals. I understood how to speak about each ring in a way that feels natural yet professional.

Download Video

Week 3: 25-Word Self-Introduction

Week 3 focused on being short, crisp, and to the point. I learned how to give a powerful introduction using only 25 words. This kind of intro is helpful in situations like networking events, elevator pitches, or quick interview rounds. The idea was to cut out unnecessary details and focus only on what matters most: my name, experience, and job-related skills. It taught me how to make an impact in very few words.

Download Video

Week 4: Resume Building

In this week, I focused on how to write a professional resume that effectively highlights my strengths. I learned that a resume should include:

• Personal details like name, address, phone, LinkedIn/GitHub
• A clear and short career objective that aligns with the job
• Educational qualifications and recent projects or internships
• Skills — both technical and soft skills
• Achievements and certifications
• Hobbies, which add a personal touch

I learned that resumes are not just about listing information — they're about presenting your profile strategically to match what recruiters are looking for.

Week 5: Group Discussion (GD) Introduction

This week, I experienced how a Group Discussion works and why it's an important part of selection processes. I learned that GD is not just about speaking a lot, but about speaking logically, listening actively, and showing team spirit.

We practiced on topics like Women Empowerment, where we initially supported women's rights. But as the discussion grew, we realized that true empowerment comes from supporting both genders, including men in areas like mental health and workplace rights.

This activity made me understand how perspectives can evolve through healthy discussion and respectful communication.

Download Video

Weeks 6-8: Group Discussions (Advanced Practice)

These weeks were focused on regular practice of Group Discussions. We were given a variety of topics and practiced starting a GD, entering at the right moment, giving examples, and summarizing at the end. I improved my confidence, fluency, and critical thinking during discussions.

I also learned to respect others' opinions and not interrupt, which are key qualities in teamwork. Each session helped me sharpen my communication style — learning when to speak, what to say, and how to support my points with facts and logic.

Download Video

Weeks 9-10: Personal Interview Practice

In these weeks, I prepared for and practiced personal interviews. I learned how to answer common questions like:

• "Tell me about yourself"
• "What are your strengths and weaknesses?"
• "Why should we hire you?"
• "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"

I worked on my body language, eye contact, and confidence level while speaking. I realized how important it is to show honesty, clarity, and enthusiasm in an interview. I also got feedback on how to improve — like giving examples from real projects, being more precise, and avoiding filler words.
